Explore places in palakkad

Palakkad Fort

Also known as Tipu’s Fort, the Palakkad Fort was built in 1766 by Hyder Ali, the Sultan of Mysore. The British later took control of it in 1790. With its well-preserved granite walls and an expansive moat, the fort is a historical monument that now hosts gardens, a children’s park, and an open-air auditorium. It’s a favorite among history enthusiasts and locals alike.

Malampuzha Dam and Gardens

The Malampuzha Dam, built across the Bharathapuzha River, is the largest reservoir in Kerala. The surrounding Malampuzha Gardens feature beautifully landscaped lawns, flower beds, fountains, and sculptures, including the famous Yakshi (a stone sculpture by renowned artist Kanayi Kunhiraman). The area also includes a rock garden, an aquarium, and a ropeway offering panoramic views of the gardens and dam.

Silent Valley National Park

A UNESCO-recognized biosphere reserve, the Silent Valley National Park is one of India’s last undisturbed tracts of tropical rainforest. Located in the Nilgiri Hills, it’s a biodiversity hotspot, home to endangered species like the lion-tailed macaque, Nilgiri tahr, and rare orchids. This park is ideal for nature lovers and trekkers, with its pristine landscapes and diverse flora and fauna

Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary

Situated on the Kerala-Tamil Nadu border, Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary is a well-protected region renowned for its population of Bengal tigers, elephants, and the rare gaur (Indian bison). The sanctuary has several dams, like the Parambikulam Dam, which adds to the scenic beauty. Guided safaris, bamboo rafting, and eco-tourism packages make it a popular spot for wildlife enthusiasts.

Kalpathy

Kalpathy is famous for the Kalpathy Ratholsavam, an annual chariot festival held at the 700-year-old Kalpathy Viswanatha Swamy Temple. Known as Kerala’s first heritage village, Kalpathy showcases Brahmin agraharam (traditional Tamil Brahmin village) architecture and customs, with charming narrow lanes, ancient homes, and the vibrant temple

Nelliyampathy Hills

Known as the “Ooty of Kerala,” the Nelliyampathy Hills offer a cool climate, lush tea and coffee plantations, and breathtaking views of the valleys below. The hills are popular for trekking and sightseeing, with viewpoints like Seetharkundu offering spectacular vistas. It’s also home to rare wildlife species and vibrant flora, making it a nature lover’s haven

Dhoni Waterfalls and Hills

Located near Palakkad town, Dhoni is known for its picturesque waterfalls and surrounding hills. A 4 km trek through the forest leads to the falls, providing an adventurous experience. The area is rich in biodiversity, and the peaceful environment makes it a great escape for hikers and nature lovers.
